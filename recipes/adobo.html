Pinoy Chicken Adobo

Pinoy Chicken Adobo is a popular Filipino dish known for its savory, tangy, and slightly sweet flavor profile. It typically consists of chicken pieces marinated and simmered in a mixture of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and black peppercorns until tender. The dish is often enjoyed with steamed rice and can be found in various versions across the Philippines, with variations in ingredients and cooking methods depending on regional preferences. It's a comforting and flavorful dish that's beloved by many Filipinos and has gained popularity internationally for its delicious taste and simplicity.

Ingredients:

  • Chicken pieces (such as drumstricks, thighs, or a whole chicken, cut into servinc pieces)
  • Soy sauce
  • Vinegar (usually cane vinegar or white vinegar)
  • Garlic (minced or crushed)
  • Bay leaves (dried or fresh)
  • Whole black peppercorns (or ground black pepper)
  • Brown sugar (usually vegetable oil)
  • Cooking oil (usually vegetable oil)
  • Water or chicken broth (for simmering)
  • Salt (to taste)

Steps:

  1. Prepare the Chicken:
    • Choose chicken pieces such as drumsticks, thighs, or a whole chicken cut into serving pieces. Ensure the chicken is cleaned and trimmed of excess fat if desired.

  2. Marinate the Chicken:
    • In a bowl, combine soy sauce, vinegar, minced or crushed garlic, whole black peppercorns, and dried or fresh bay leaves.
    • Place the chicken pieces in the marinade, ensuring they are well-coated. You can marinate the chicken for at least 30 minutes to several hours, or even overnight in the refrigerator for more flavor.

  3. Cooking the Chicken Adobo:
    • In a large pan or pot, heat cooking oil over medium heat.
    • Once the oil is hot, add the marinated chicken pieces (reserve the marinade) and cook until lightly browned on all sides, about 5-7 minutes.
    • Pour in the reserved marinade and add water or chicken broth to cover the chicken.
    • Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer, covered, for about 30-40 minutes or until the chicken is tender and fully cooked.
    • Optionally, you can add brown sugar to the simmering sauce for a slightly sweet flavor, adjusting the amount to your taste preference.

  4. Adjust Seasoning and Serve:
    • Taste the sauce and adjust the seasoning with salt if needed.
    • Once the chicken is cooked and the sauce has thickened to your desired consistency, remove the bay leaves and whole peppercorns.
    • Serve the Pinoy Chicken Adobo hot with steamed rice. Optionally, garnish with chopped green onions or fresh parsley for added flavor and presentation.

Enjoy eating your Chicken Adobo! This is the best when partnered with rice.
